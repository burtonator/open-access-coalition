Our goal is to fight for the open web!

What is the open web?

The open web is based on the concept that websites participate fairly with one another and allow data to be published and accessed without being blocked by a walled garden which otherwise prevents access to the data.

A real world example is the way Google fetches content from websites on the Internet and was able to build a billion dollar industry on data posted publicly.

Much of this data is published within the public domain, available under fair use, or Creative Commons license.

Problems arise when this data is subsequently blocked from being accessed by 3rd parties even when the 3rd party has a license for the data.

What is non-discriminatory access?

A major component to a fair access policy is non-discriminatory access. It's paramount that a hosting provider treat each party in an identical manner. The access rules must remain consistent.

We have seen this applied in the past where a hosting provider will cut off API access to a company should they decide that they are making too much revenue or that their product is now competing with something they want to offer.

This is a chilling effect becuase it prevents companies from investing significantly in an API because they may be cut off at any time.

With out non-discriminatory access, the web is not open.
